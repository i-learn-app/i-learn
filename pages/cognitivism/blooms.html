Definition


Created by psychologist Dr. Benjamin Bloom in 1956.To promote higher forms of thinking in education, such as analyzing and evaluating concepts, processes, procedures, and principles, rather than just remembering facts (rote learning). Educators usually used blooms to guide them in development assessments example test and other evaluation of student learning.
